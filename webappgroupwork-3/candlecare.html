Candle Care

1. Trim the Wick

Before lighting your candle, always trim the wick to about Â¼ inch (0.6 cm) in length. This helps to prevent excessive flickering, sooting, and uneven burning. Use a wick trimmer or scissors to trim the wick.

2. Allow for Proper Burn Time

When lighting a candle for the first time, allow it to burn until the wax melts evenly across the entire surface. This helps to prevent tunneling and ensures the candle burns evenly in subsequent uses.

3. Avoid Drafts

Place your candles in a draft-free area to ensure an even and steady burn. Drafts can cause the flame to flicker, resulting in uneven burning and potential wax drips.

4. Burn in Intervals

To maximize the lifespan of your candle, burn it in intervals of 2-3 hours. This prevents the candle from overheating and helps to avoid excessive sooting. Remember to extinguish the candle between burn sessions.

5. Extinguish Safely

Always extinguish candles using a proper candle snuffer or by gently blowing out the flame. Avoid using water to extinguish the candle, as it can cause hot wax to splatter.

6. Keep the Wax Pool Clean

After extinguishing the flame, ensure that the wax pool is free from debris such as wick trimmings or matches. This helps to maintain a clean burn and prevents any potential hazards.

7. Store Candles Properly

Store your candles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight or extreme temperatures. This helps to preserve their color, fragrance, and overall quality.

To ensure a safe candle-burning experience, here are some important safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Never Leave Unattended
  • Keep Away from Flammable Objects
  • Use Candle Holders or Trays
  • Avoid Overcrowding

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long should I burn my candle for?

A: It is recommended to burn candles for 2-3 hours at a time to prevent overheating and ensure an even burn.

Q: Can I relight a partially burned candle?

A: Yes, you can relight a partially burned candle. However, make sure to trim the wick before relighting and allow the wax to melt evenly across the surface to avoid tunneling.

Q: How do I prevent my candles from producing soot?

A: To prevent excessive sooting, make sure to trim the wick to the recommended length before each use. Avoid placing candles in drafty areas, as drafts can cause the flame to flicker and produce soot.

Q: What should I do if my candle is burning unevenly?

A: If your candle is burning unevenly, gently reposition the wick towards the center while the wax is still melted and the flame is extinguished. This helps to ensure an even burn in the future.

Q: How do I clean the wax residue from the candle container?

A: To clean wax residue from the container, place it in the freezer for a few hours. Once the wax hardens, you can easily remove it with a butter knife or gently tap the container to release the wax.
